## Summary

Ramp (founded 2019, 50,000+ customers, $100B+ annual purchases, backed by Sequoia/Founders Fund/Khosla/Lightspeed) is a financial operations platform combining payments, corporate cards, vendor management, procurement, and automated bookkeeping. This Senior Software Engineer role sits on Ramp's Web Engineering team focused on the GTM Platform — building AI-powered frontend apps that supercharge customer acquisition and marketing workflows. Not a traditional GTM Engineer role: this is a frontend engineering position (React/Next.js/Tailwind) that builds tools marketers use, including autonomous agents for paid ad experiments and LLM copilots for sales collateral.

## Responsibilities

- Build AI-powered frontend apps to assist marketer workflows (self-serve landing page prototyper, autonomous paid ad experiment agent, LLM copilot for sales collateral)
- Collaborate with design, growth, and marketing on website projects that maximize qualified leads across GTM channels
- Build design systems and foster a culture of industry-leading UX
- Continuously improve engineering processes, tools, and systems to scale the codebase, productivity, and team

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- Strong proficiency in React
- Minimum 3 years of frontend engineering experience
- Experience applying AI/LLMs in production environments
- Desire to work in a fast-paced environment, continuously grow, and master your craft

### Nice-to-Have
- Experience with Next.js
- Experience with Tailwind CSS
- Strong perspective on the engineering development lifecycle (system design, documentation, testing, long-term codebase health)

## Key Concepts

- **GTM Platform Engineering**: A dedicated engineering team that builds internal tools and systems specifically to power go-to-market functions (marketing, sales, growth). Unlike a GTM Engineer who configures existing tools, this team writes production software that marketers use — blurring the line between product engineering and growth.
- **Self-Serve Landing Page Prototyper**: An internal tool that lets marketers create and test landing pages without engineering support — an example of the AI-powered apps this role builds, enabling faster experimentation cycles for conversion optimization.
