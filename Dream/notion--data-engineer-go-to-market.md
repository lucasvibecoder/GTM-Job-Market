## Summary
Notion is a productivity platform (docs, projects, calendar, email with AI built in) used by millions including Toyota, Figma, and OpenAI. The Data Engineer, Go-To-Market builds foundational datasets and pipelines powering marketing and sales reporting — integrating product data with business systems like Salesforce and Marketo. Heavy data engineering role supporting GTM strategy, not a GTM Engineer.

## Responsibilities
- Build core datasets as sources of truth for Notion's marketing and sales reporting
- Integrate data to and from business systems and Notion's product
- Partner with Marketing, Sales, Revenue Operations, Business Technology, BI, and Data Science teams
- Design, build, and monitor pipelines that scale with growing data size
- Democratize access to high-quality data across GTM teams and the entire company

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 3+ years as a data engineer building core datasets, ideally with high data volumes
- Experience with Marketing and Sales datasets at a SaaS company
- Built integrations with business systems (Salesforce, Netsuite, Marketo, Zendesk)
- Self-starter who gathers needs from business partners and designs technical solutions
- Scalable data solutions in the cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure) across multiple data stores (Snowflake, Redshift, Hive)
- SQL expert — aggregation functions, window functions, UDFs, self-joins, partitioning, clustering
- Comfortable with object-oriented programming (Python, Java, Scala)
- Curious about AI tools and willing to adopt them

### Nice-to-Have
- Scalable data pipelines using BigData stack (Airflow, dbt, Spark, Hive, Parquet/ORC)
- Experience working with GTM stakeholders
- Fast-growing company experience

## Key Concepts
- **Dimensional modeling**: A data warehouse design approach using fact and dimension tables (star/snowflake schemas) to organize business data for fast analytical queries. Core methodology this role uses to structure GTM data.
- **Data democratization**: Making high-quality data accessible to non-technical GTM teams so they can self-serve reporting and analysis. The strategic outcome this role enables beyond just building pipelines.
