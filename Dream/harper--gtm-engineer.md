## Summary

Harper (YC W25) is an AI-powered commercial insurance platform that's grown 100x year-over-year, adding ~1,000 customers/month. This GTM Engineer role owns the metrics, analytics, and experimentation infrastructure that powers growth — building KPI systems, LTV/CAC tracking, multi-touch attribution, and paid channel analytics across Google Ads, Meta, and TikTok. Ship production code that directly moves revenue.

> **Title note:** Listed as "GTM Engineer" on the LinkedIn posting, but the URL slug reads "data-scientist-gtm" — likely reflecting how Harper initially categorized the role internally. The JD content skews heavily toward analytics engineering and data science. Both labels are useful context as the market figures out naming for these hybrid roles.

## Responsibilities

- Build metrics and KPI infrastructure — define, instrument, and own the metrics that matter in real time
- Own LTV/CAC systems — track unit economics across verticals, channels, and cohorts
- Build paid channel analytics — connect ad spend to actual revenue, not vanity metrics
- Create multi-touch attribution across voice, email, web, and referral
- Use AI for insight generation — pattern detection, anomaly detection, automated analysis
- Ship experimentation infrastructure — A/B testing with statistical rigor

## Requirements

### Must-Have

- 2-5 years experience in analytics engineering or growth engineering
- Strong Python and SQL skills
- Experience with paid acquisition data and funnel analytics
- Ability to ship production code, not just analysis
- Experience with A/B testing and experimentation
- Based in San Francisco or willing to relocate

### Nice-to-Have

- Experience with PostHog, Amplitude, or similar product analytics
- Background in lead scoring or attribution modeling
- Prior startup or high-growth company experience

## Key Concepts

- **LTV/CAC Analysis**: Comparing a customer's lifetime value against the cost to acquire them — the core unit economics that determine whether a growth engine is sustainable. This role builds the systems to track these metrics across verticals and channels.
- **Multi-Touch Attribution**: A measurement approach that assigns credit to multiple marketing touchpoints (voice, email, web, referral) in a customer's journey, rather than giving all credit to the first or last touch. Critical for understanding which channels actually drive revenue.
