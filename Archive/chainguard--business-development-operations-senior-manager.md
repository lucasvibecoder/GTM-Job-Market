## Summary
Chainguard provides secure open source software (backed by major investors, customers include enterprise security-focused orgs). The BDR Ops Senior Manager enables effectiveness, efficiency, and scalability of the Business Development org. Partners with BDR Leadership, RevOps, Finance, Sales, Marketing, and GTM Enablement. Not a GTM Engineer title but heavily GTME-adjacent — owns automation, AI strategy, and pipeline generation tooling.

## Responsibilities
- Design, optimize, and scale all global BDR operational workflows (inbound and outbound)
- Define, track, and analyze BDR metrics: funnel performance, rep productivity, workflow health
- Build capacity and productivity models, partner on headcount prioritization, activity pacing, pipeline coverage
- Support AI strategy for pipeline generation: automation and personalization across research, prospecting, and activity pillars
- Optimize BDR tech stack (Salesforce, LinkedIn Sales Nav, Nooks, ZoomInfo, Clay, Cognism, Outreach)
- Develop documentation and best practices for BDR systems
- Own BDR issue management: access, lead routing, Salesforce corrections, tooling troubleshooting
- Facilitate monthly BDR quota audit process
- Build Salesforce and Sigma dashboards for funnel performance and rep productivity
- Design BDR SPIFF and incentive programs

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 5+ years in Sales Strategy, Revenue Ops, or GTM Ops (B2B SaaS)
- Pipeline operating cadences (forecasting, pipeline reviews, QBRs) and strategic GTM planning
- Capacity modeling, org design, quota setting
- Google Sheets proficiency and data visualization (Sigma)
- Salesforce admin and common GTM tools
- Attention to detail, organizational skills, multi-priority management
- Strong communicator, comfortable presenting to cross-functional stakeholders
- Adaptable in fast-paced, high-growth environments

### Nice-to-Have
- Supporting 50+ rep global BDR org through hyper-growth
- GTM AI tooling for scalable pipeline generation

## Key Concepts
- **BDR operations at scale**: Operating the machinery (processes, tools, metrics, comp plans) that makes a 50+ rep BDR org productive. Not building outbound — building the system that manages outbound.
- **Capacity modeling**: Forecasting how many reps are needed, what their activity targets should be, and how pipeline coverage maps to revenue goals.
