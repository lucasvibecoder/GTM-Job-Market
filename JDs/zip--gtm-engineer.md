## Summary
Zip is a $2.2B procurement platform (Y Combinator, Tiger Global, BOND) used by OpenAI, Snowflake, Anthropic, Coinbase. This is the founding GTM Engineer role — a hybrid of RevOps technologist, product manager, and automation architect. Defines a new discipline at Zip with high autonomy across Sales and Marketing Ops, Business Systems, and Sales Leadership.

## Responsibilities
- Build the GTM automation engine: scalable, high-velocity workflows for rep efficiency and pipeline generation
- Use Clay and n8n for event-based workflows: data enrichment, account routing, personalized outreach at scale
- Operationalize AI and enrichment tools to boost outbound quality and personalization
- Be product owner for GTM stack: manage requirements, prioritization, system rollouts
- Define and track metrics for sales productivity, funnel velocity, automation ROI
- Build dashboards and data models for visible, actionable performance
- Partner across GTM Ops for data consistency, routing logic, attribution accuracy
- Pilot new tools, integrations, and AI workflows

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 4+ years in GTM systems, RevOps engineering, or product management (B2B SaaS)
- Salesforce experience (Contact, Account, Opportunity, Campaign objects)
- Strong SQL, data modeling, and at least one programming language (Python, JavaScript, Apex)
- LLM applications and prompting at scale
- Complex integrations and workflow automation (Workato, Zapier, Clay, n8n, custom scripts, CDP/reverse ETL like Hightouch, Census)
- Strategic mindset: business needs → system design → execution
- Excellent written and verbal communication
- High bias for action, low ego, relentless ownership

### Nice-to-Have
- None explicitly listed

## Key Concepts
- **Founding GTM Engineer**: First person dedicated to engineering the GTM systems at a $2.2B company. Rare opportunity to define the discipline and infrastructure from scratch.
- **Event-based GTM workflows**: Using Clay and n8n to trigger actions based on events (signals, data changes, account behavior) rather than batch processes.
