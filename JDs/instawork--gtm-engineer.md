## Summary

Instawork is the nation's leading AI-powered labor marketplace connecting 7M+ skilled hourly workers with businesses across 50+ US/Canada markets. This GTM Engineer role blends RevOps and GTM engineering with heavy AI usage — building automation tools, owning revenue systems, and driving commercial projects from idea to impact. Reports to VP of Business Operations as an IC with growth potential into people management. **Note:** LinkedIn URL slug lists this as "GTM Operations and Automation Manager" but the JD body consistently uses "GTM Engineer."

## Responsibilities

- Build GTM automation tools to increase sales team efficiency and 10x specific sales motions
- Architect Python notebooks, n8n workflows, and other tools (referral identification systems, lead scoring models)
- Own revenue systems and core GTM processes including pipeline tracking, process optimization, lead enrichment
- Maintain and enhance revenue tech stack (CRM, automation tools, data platforms)
- Evaluate commercial strategy to surface gaps and drive prioritization across the organization
- Partner with Sales and Product to ship GTM innovations with high velocity and ownership

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 5+ years experience in revenue operations, GTM engineering, data analytics, management consulting, or similar
- Proficiency in Python and SQL
- Experience with API integration
- Obsession with automation and eliminating manual work

### Nice-to-Have
- Experience with GTM tech stack (Salesforce, Outreach, Clay)
- Experience with AI code copilots (Claude Code, Cursor)

## Key Concepts

- **Labor Marketplace**: Platform connecting hourly workers with businesses needing shift-based staffing — Instawork's core product spanning food services, hospitality, light industry, and logistics.
- **Lead Scoring Model**: Algorithmic system that ranks prospects by likelihood to convert, enabling sales to prioritize high-value leads over manual prospecting.
- **n8n Workflows**: Open-source workflow automation tool used here to orchestrate GTM processes — similar to Zapier/Make but with more developer control via code nodes.
