## Summary

Root Layer is a sales enablement consultancy that builds outbound machines for clients — powered by lived experience and enhanced by AI. This GTM Engineer is a **1099 contractor** ($1,500-$12,000/month depending on client load, annualized in salary fields) who identifies total market opportunity and builds the outbound engine for each client: market segmentation, buyer intent signals, targeted lists, and campaign management. Must demo Clay, Cursor, and Claude Code live to the founder. Client-facing, consulting model.

## Responsibilities

- Identify total market opportunity and build the outbound machine for each client
- Segment and rank addressable portions of the market
- Back segmentation with buyer intent signals at the intersection of client's offer and customer pain points
- Develop targeted lists of potential buyers
- Manage outbound campaigns to those buyers
- Work directly with Sales Specialists and AI tools to connect with prospective buyers
- Get meetings to the right person within the client's organization
- Ensure all tools are connected throughout the stack of internal and outreach systems

## Requirements

### Must-Have

- Live demonstration of working/thinking with Clay, Cursor, and Claude Code
- Understanding APIs and thinking in data flows
- Working with CRMs like HubSpot or Salesforce
- Mass outbound management and domain management
- Ability to have a value-added conversation with the founder on tooling and client outcomes
- Scrappy builder mindset — balance custom builds vs off-the-shelf tools, iterate on feedback

### Nice-to-Have

- Prior inside sales experience

## Key Concepts

- **Reach vs Relevance**: The distinction between sending high volumes of outreach (reach) and sending targeted, resonant messages to the right buyers (relevance). Root Layer's thesis is that scaling what doesn't work just creates more noise — you need to validate the motion with real experience before automating it.
- **1099 GTM Engineering**: A contractor/freelance model where GTM Engineers are paid per client engagement rather than salaried. Compensation scales with client load, creating a direct link between capacity and income. Emerging pattern as more consultancies productize GTM engineering services.
