## Summary
Outlier is a consumer-first sports data and betting insights platform helping bettors make smarter decisions. The GTM/Growth Engineer builds and operates the marketing and growth infrastructure — data pipelines, attribution systems, experimentation platform, landing pages, and ad tech integrations. Reports to CMO. Heavy engineering role focused on turning technical leverage into measurable growth.

## Responsibilities
- Own the architecture, implementation, and maintenance of the marketing tech stack (CRMs, analytics platforms, tag managers, ad integrations)
- Evaluate, onboard, and deprecate tools with a focus on data quality, reliability, and ROI
- Design and implement a comprehensive event taxonomy and tracking architecture across web and mobile
- Build and maintain data pipelines feeding clean user behavior data into analytics and marketing systems
- Develop and maintain the experimentation platform (A/B testing, feature flags, holdout groups) at scale
- Advise teams on test design and statistical validity
- Identify and instrument conversion bottlenecks across acquisition and onboarding funnels
- Implement landing page optimization, funnel automation, and real-time personalization
- Manage technical integrations with paid media platforms (Meta, Google) including pixel implementation and server-side event APIs
- Ensure data quality and signal fidelity across all ad platforms
- Work closely with product engineers to build growth instrumentation into the product development process
- Translate marketing requirements into technical specifications and vice versa

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 3-5+ years of software engineering or data engineering experience
- Proficiency in at least one backend language
- Solid understanding of APIs, webhooks, and event-driven architectures
- Hands-on experience with Braze, Google Tag Manager, Mixpanel, and major ad platform APIs
- Experience building and maintaining data pipelines and working with event schemas
- Working knowledge of attribution modeling (last-touch, multi-touch, incrementality)
- Experience implementing server-side conversion APIs
- Experience building or operating A/B testing frameworks with statistical rigor
- Ability to translate between marketing strategy and engineering implementation
- Comfortable in fast-moving startup environments

### Nice-to-Have
- Data warehouse familiarity

## Key Concepts
- **Event taxonomy**: A structured naming and tracking architecture for user actions across web and mobile. The foundation that makes all downstream analytics and marketing automation possible.
- **Server-side conversion APIs**: Sending conversion events directly from the server to ad platforms (Meta CAPI, Google offline conversions) instead of relying on browser pixels — critical for attribution accuracy as cookies decline.
- **Holdout groups**: A segment of users deliberately excluded from an experiment to measure its true incremental impact. Essential for rigorous A/B testing beyond simple A/B splits.
