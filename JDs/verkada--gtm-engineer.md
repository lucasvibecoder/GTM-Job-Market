## Summary
Verkada is a cloud physical security platform (30,000+ orgs, 100+ Fortune 500 customers, 2,200+ employees) with solutions for video security, access control, sensors, and visitor management. The GTM Engineer sits on the Growth Engineering team, building backend infrastructure to power Verkada's growth engine — experimentation systems, purchase journey processing, and marketing funnel optimization. This is a software engineering role that happens to focus on growth; requires a CS degree and 3+ years SWE experience. In-office 5 days/week at San Mateo HQ.

## Responsibilities
- Design and implement reliable, observable infrastructure powering the growth engine
- Maintain and enhance backend systems to process the purchase journey and optimize automated workflows
- Build multivariate experimentation infrastructure to improve marketing funnel efficiency
- Integrate new technologies and platforms with existing infrastructure
- Work cross-functionally with Sales, Marketing, and Product to translate business requirements into features
- Drive innovation in marketing technology, improving efficiency and effectiveness

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or similar
- 3+ years of experience in Software Engineering using backend technologies (Python or similar)
- Experience owning software architecture and API design
- Ability to learn new systems and technologies quickly
- Strong interest in communicating cross-functionally with non-technical stakeholders

### Nice-to-Have
- Proactively experimenting with new technologies for traditional business challenges
- Experience shipping large-scale, highly visible, external-facing features/products

## Key Concepts
- **Growth engineering**: A software engineering discipline focused on building infrastructure for user acquisition, conversion optimization, and revenue growth — metrics center on business outcomes rather than product features.
- **Multivariate experimentation infrastructure**: Systems enabling simultaneous testing of multiple variables across the marketing funnel, optimizing conversion rates with statistical rigor.
