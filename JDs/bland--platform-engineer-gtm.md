## Summary

Bland AI (Series B, $65M raised) builds AI phone agents for customer communication at scale. This Platform Engineer (GTM) role sits on a centralized platform team owning all internal systems. Starts with GTM infrastructure — designing HubSpot data models and building Clay enrichment/outbound automation — then expands into APIs, integration reliability, observability, and internal tooling across the company. Entry-level role targeting new grads.

> **Title note:** Listed as "Platform Engineer (GTM)" — not a GTM Engineer variant but a platform engineering role that starts with GTM infrastructure as its first domain. Marked `gtm_adjacent`.

## Responsibilities

- Design and own the HubSpot data model (objects, workflows, scoring, reporting)
- Build enrichment and outbound automation in Clay
- Write scripts and services to integrate product data into revenue systems
- Build dashboards and data pipelines for full funnel visibility
- Design and implement APIs and automation connecting revenue, finance, and internal systems
- Eliminate manual workflows with code, not process
- Debug and refactor brittle integrations for long-term reliability
- Implement logging, retries, and error handling across integrations
- Monitor systems for data drift and silent failure
- Improve internal observability and incident workflows
- Integrate identity and access controls into internal systems
- Build infrastructure that scales without increasing headcount

## Requirements

### Must-Have

- Graduating or recently graduated in CS or similar technical field

- Can take an ambiguous problem, design an approach, and unblock yourself without hand-holding
- Bias toward shipping — move fast without cutting corners on quality

### Nice-to-Have

## Key Concepts

- **Platform Engineering (GTM context)**: A centralized engineering function that owns all internal systems — revenue tooling, HR, finance, security, observability — through one intake and roadmap. Different from a dedicated GTM Engineer in that GTM is one domain among many, not the entire scope.
- **Data Drift**: When data in integrated systems gradually becomes inconsistent or inaccurate over time — fields go stale, mappings break silently, deduplication fails. This role monitors for and prevents it across the integration layer.
