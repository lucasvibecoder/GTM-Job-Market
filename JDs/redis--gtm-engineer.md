## Summary

Redis is the in-memory data store powering fast applications for 10,000+ customers worldwide. This GTM Engineer sits on a small team focused on removing friction from how Redis goes to market — building workflows, automations, and AI-powered tools for Marketing and SDR teams. Equal parts product manager and engineer: you own the full lifecycle from discovering pain points to shipping and iterating solutions that drive pipeline and velocity.

## Responsibilities

- Partner with Marketing and SDR teams to identify, scope, and prioritize high-impact automation and AI opportunities
- Design and build workflows, automations, and data pipelines that make Marketing and SDR teams faster and more productive
- Explore APIs, SDKs, and emerging AI tools to test new ways of accelerating GTM execution
- Translate customer needs into features while building the engineering solutions
- Balance speed with durability — partner with operations and systems teams to ensure solutions are secure, maintainable, and scalable
- Operate with autonomy, owning projects from problem discovery through solution delivery and iteration

## Requirements

### Must-Have

- 2-5 years building or supporting GTM systems in GTM Engineering, Business Technology, or Solutions Engineering
- Hands-on AI, automation, or API experience with concrete examples
- Familiarity with modern AI and automation tools (OpenAI, Cursor, n8n, v0/Vercel, Glean)
- Strong problem-solving skills and comfort with ambiguity
- Bias for action — move from idea to experiment to solution quickly
- Clear communicator across technical and non-technical audiences
- Thrives in small, high-growth environments with significant ownership

### Nice-to-Have

- Prototype- or production-level code in Python, JavaScript/TypeScript
- Experience with GTM platforms (Salesforce, Marketo, Pocus, Asana)
- Exposure to experimentation, analytics, or performance measurement in GTM context

## Key Concepts

- **GTM Engineering as Product Management**: Treating internal GTM stakeholders (Marketing, SDRs) as customers — discovering their pain points, defining "better," designing solutions, and iterating based on outcomes. The engineer owns the full product lifecycle for internal tools.
- **High-Leverage Automation**: Identifying the specific friction points where automation delivers outsized returns on effort — not automating everything, but finding the 20% of workflows that drive 80% of pipeline impact.
