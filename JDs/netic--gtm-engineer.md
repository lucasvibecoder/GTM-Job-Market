## Summary
Netic is the AI revenue engine for essential services ($43M raised from Founders Fund, Greylock, Hanabi, Dylan Field). The GTM Engineer will build the entire GTM engine from scratch — TAM mapping, intent signals, tool selection (buy vs. build), and internal automations for sales and marketing. Unique angle: you also help Netic's customers build their GTM engines.

## Responsibilities
- Level up how Netic maps the essential service TAM across the US
- Design, test, and monitor intent signals against the company database
- Help inform Netic's early tech stack decisions
- Build foundational internal tools, workflows, and automations for sales and marketing to leverage AI
- Apply GTM Engineering principles to help customers be more successful
- Build a flywheel based on customer conversations to accelerate user understanding
- Dig into customer usage data to inform top-of-funnel campaigns and messaging
- Chip in wherever needed as the team scales

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 3+ years in GTM Engineering, sales, growth, RevOps, or MarketingOps
- Strong technical foundations — can write code and architect scalable systems
- Great business intuition with demonstrated metric-moving experience
- Deep curiosity — tinkers in free time, runs down rabbit holes
- Hustle and grit — thrives in ambiguity, iterates quickly
- Exceptional cross-functional skills — builds strong partnerships with Sales and internal teams
- High-ownership leadership — sets the standard, takes full accountability

### Nice-to-Have
- None explicitly listed

## Key Concepts
- **TAM mapping**: Total Addressable Market definition — identifying and segmenting every potential customer in a market. The foundation for all outbound and signal-based GTM.
- **Buy vs. build (GTM stack)**: The strategic decision of whether to purchase existing tools or build custom solutions. Critical for early-stage companies defining their tech stack from scratch.
