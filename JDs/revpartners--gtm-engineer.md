## Summary
RevPartners is a HubSpot consulting partner (fastest-growing HubSpot partner globally) that engineers revenue outcomes for GTM leaders. The GTM Engineer powers the Allbound Engine for RP's clients — building and optimizing Clay-based automation workflows, connecting enrichment and outreach tools, and ensuring data integrity for intent-based campaigns. Consulting context: you build outbound infrastructure for multiple client accounts, not just one internal team.

## Responsibilities
- Build and optimize Claybook processes based on RP's Allbound Maturity Model
- Connect and orchestrate tools such as Clay, HubSpot, Smartlead, and Outbound Sync
- Ensure data integrity and accuracy for campaign execution
- Design new and creative ways to unlock value through data and tech stack
- Partner with Allbound and RevOps teams to support all aspects of GTM campaigns
- Coach junior team members on automation best practices and platform fluency

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 2+ years building complex data enrichment and automation workflows
- 1+ years advanced experience with Clay
- Strong understanding of Clay's integration capabilities and limitations
- Hands-on experience with web scraping tools and techniques
- Proficient in manipulating data formats (CSV, etc.)
- Skilled in data cleaning and validation processes
- Proficiency in remote communication (Google Meet, Slack, email)

### Nice-to-Have
- Agency background
- HubSpot experience across Marketing, Sales, CMS, and Ops Hub
- Familiarity with REST APIs, webhooks, and custom integrations
- Experience with ClickUp, Vector/RB2B, Copy.ai, SmartLead/Lemlist, Ads Platforms, Chili Piper, Sendos

## Key Concepts
- **Allbound Engine**: RevPartners' framework for combining inbound and outbound motions into a unified pipeline strategy for their clients. The GTM Engineer builds the technical automation layer that powers this.
- **Claybook Processes**: Standardized Clay workflow templates that map to different maturity levels of a client's outbound infrastructure. Think of them as playbooks implemented in Clay.
