## Summary

Sapien is building toward an "autonomous CFO" — an AI platform that runs company financials end-to-end for traditional businesses (manufacturing, healthcare, restaurants, enterprises). Seed-stage ($9M raised, backed by General Catalyst and Neo, HQ in NYC). This GTM Engineer is the engineering owner of customer success — building internal sales tools, setting up customer modules and data pipelines, owning integrations with customer systems (ERPs, CRMs, accounting software), and being the DRI for whether prospects convert and customers expand. Full-stack role (React, TypeScript, Python, SQL) split between internal tooling and hands-on customer work.

## Responsibilities

- Build internal tools that accelerate the sales motion — demo generation, automated onboarding, interfaces showing technical blockers
- Own customer module setup from technical sale through production — data upload/validation, workflow configuration, custom integrations
- Build and maintain integrations with customer systems (ERPs, CRMs, accounting software, proprietary tools) — authentication, data syncing, schema mapping
- Be the DRI for customer performance — monitor accuracy and reliability, debug issues, optimize slow queries/workflows
- Design product features that turn pilots into successful deployments, working with product team on expansion and churn prevention
- Identify patterns across customers that should become platform capabilities — turn one-off integrations into reusable connectors
- Build scalable customer operations tooling — data validation, integration testing, module templates, monitoring

## Requirements

### Must-Have

- Full-stack engineering experience with React, TypeScript, Python, and SQL
- Customer-facing experience or strong interest in working directly with customers
- Bias toward pragmatic solutions over perfect architecture — shipping beats over-engineering
- Ownership mentality for customer outcomes
- Ability to operate with ambiguity and execute without detailed specs

### Nice-to-Have

- None listed

## Key Concepts

- **Technical DRI for Customer Success**: A model where a single engineer owns the full customer lifecycle from technical sale through production — not just building tools, but being directly accountable for whether customers convert, get value, and expand. Blurs the line between Solutions Engineering, Customer Success, and Product Engineering.
- **One-off to Platform Pattern**: The discipline of recognizing when custom integrations, scripts, or manual setup work done for individual customers should be abstracted into reusable platform capabilities — connectors, templates, automated flows — to make customer operations scalable.
