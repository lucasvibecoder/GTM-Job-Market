## Summary

**LiveFlow** builds a next-generation accounting and finance platform for lean finance teams running massive enterprises. Raised $21M+ from YC, YC Continuity, Valar, Seedcamp, and others — founding team ex-Revolut. This **GTM Engineer** owns new AI initiatives driving go-to-market strategy for CFOs, working directly under the CEO and COO. Heavy emphasis on hypothesis-driven analysis, cross-functional project leadership, and automating processes with AI. Background skews strategy/consulting rather than pure engineering.

## Responsibilities

- Develop a thorough understanding of LiveFlow's ICP
- Create reports to extract actionable metrics and inform decisions for GTM and content strategy
- Identify key 0-to-1 initiatives and expand the role by forming hypotheses, conducting analysis, and delivering executive-level proposals
- Drive efforts across Sales, Customer Success, Business Development, RevOps, and Product to problem-solve, build, and lead projects fueling growth
- Stay adaptable and open to new opportunities directly under CEO and COO

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- At least 2 years of experience in a fast-paced environment (investment banking, private equity, management consulting, early-stage startups)
- Excellent communication skills
- Familiarity with sales and marketing, or ability to learn quickly
- Comfort with ambiguity and testing/failing/learning to solve blue-sky problems
- Strong interest in and ability to stay current with emerging AI and search technologies
- Prior experience using AI tools like Clay, Claude, ChatGPT for automating workflows
- Hands-on experience moving data across systems via APIs and webhooks

### Nice-to-Have
- None listed

## Key Concepts

- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**: A detailed description of the type of company most likely to buy and succeed with the product — defined by firmographics (size, industry, revenue) and behavioral signals, used to focus GTM efforts on highest-value targets.
- **0-to-1 Initiatives**: Building something from nothing — creating a new function, channel, or process where none existed before, as opposed to optimizing an existing one.
