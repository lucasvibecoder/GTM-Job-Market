## Summary
SentiLink builds identity verification and fraud prevention APIs for financial institutions — Forbes Fintech 50, backed by a16z and Craft Ventures. This is their first GTM Engineer hire, owning the full technical backbone of the revenue engine: systems, workflows, and AI-powered tools across RevOps, Sales, Marketing, and Partner Success. High-visibility 0→1 role with direct impact on how the company scales.

## Responsibilities
- Design and maintain tools and processes to supercharge the GTM engine
- Connect and build the GTM tech stack into a cohesive, scalable system
- Identify and execute on automation opportunities across the full funnel
- Build tools to uncover and surface opportunities for revenue generation
- Partner with GTM teams to translate business needs into clean, scalable solutions
- Proactively identify process and analytics improvements across the GTM motion
- Build a system that automatically identifies data-backed sales hooks and surfaces them to reps during outreach

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 4+ years in GTM systems, revenue operations, and/or software/product engineering
- Hands-on experience with CRMs, revenue forecasting, and internal tools for non-technical teams
- SQL fluency
- Scrappy with AI tooling — LLM power user with experience building agents
- Experimental mindset: idea → prototype → production
- Experience working alongside Sales, Partner Success, and Marketing teams
- 0→1 builder who takes ownership and iterates with real-world feedback

### Nice-to-Have
- Fintech, fraud, identity, or regulated B2B background
- Salesforce experience
- Python and/or JavaScript fluency
- Clay Sales Intelligence experience

## Key Concepts
- **Signal-to-rep surfacing**: Automatically identifying compelling, data-backed sales hooks from proprietary data and putting them in reps' hands during outreach. The first project and core motion this role builds.
- **0→1 GTM infrastructure**: Building the technical backbone of a revenue engine from scratch — no existing systems to inherit, full ownership of architecture decisions and tool selection.
