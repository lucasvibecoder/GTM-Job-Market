## Summary
Aion is an AI infrastructure research lab building a full-stack platform for building, fine-tuning, and deploying AI at scale (compute orchestration, training workflows, data pipelines, model versioning, deployment). The GTM Engineer is a sales- and partnerships-focused role blending technical fluency with commercial execution — demos, POCs, customer pilots, strategic partnerships, and co-selling. Works directly with founders on high-impact deals. Hybrid (3 days in-office SF).

## Responsibilities
- Support sales conversations with technical depth — demos, architectures, proof-of-concepts
- Scope, launch, and support customer pilot deployments with startups and enterprises
- Remove technical friction from the sales cycle to accelerate deals
- Identify and build relationships with strategic partners (cloud providers, AI tooling companies, research labs)
- Design joint GTM motions with partners — co-selling, co-marketing, events, integrations, bundled offerings
- Work closely with leadership on high-impact founder-level strategic deals
- Feed real-world GTM and sales learnings back into Product and Engineering
- Define and refine ICPs, verticals, and core AI infrastructure use cases
- Own GTM KPIs tied to pipeline, conversions, deal velocity, and revenue influence

## Requirements

### Must-Have
- 3-7+ years in GTM, Sales Engineering, Partnerships, or Solutions Engineering
- Strong technical foundation (cloud, AI infra, developer tools, or data platforms)
- Comfortable discussing model training, inference, deployment, and infrastructure tradeoffs
- Commercially sharp — understands how deals get done
- Builder mindset with high ownership and urgency
- Willing to travel for customers, partners, and strategic events

### Nice-to-Have
- None explicitly listed

## Key Concepts
- **Revenue-adjacent technical motion**: A GTM approach where the engineer directly supports and accelerates sales cycles with technical depth (demos, architectures, POCs) rather than building backend systems. The bridge between product capability and commercial outcome.
- **Co-selling and co-marketing**: Partnering with strategic ecosystem players (cloud providers, AI tooling companies) to jointly sell and market, creating bundled offerings and shared pipeline. A key growth lever for infrastructure companies selling to developers and enterprises.
